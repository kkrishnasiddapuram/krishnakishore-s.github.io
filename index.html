Who I Am

Building the
Invisible World

I'm a PhD candidate in Micro Electronics and VLSI at IIT Hyderabad, currently on an exchange at the University of Tokyo's Hongo Campus. My work lives at the nanoscale — designing infrared pixel sensors that push the boundaries of what silicon can do.

From the fabrication cleanroom to simulation software, I bridge hardware and computation — using COMSOL Multiphysics for device modeling, silicon reactive ion etching for fabrication, and custom ROIC design to read out signals from devices smaller than a human hair.

I am part of the CHIPS LAB and was selected for the prestigious 2025 STARS Lite Program at Purdue University, sponsored by IIT Hyderabad and India's Ministry of Education.
